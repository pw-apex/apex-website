Background

The client.

Spot AI is a video intelligence platform headquartered in Los Altos, California. Founded in 2018 by three Stanford graduates, the company has raised $93M — including a $40M Series B led by Scale Venture Partners — and serves 1,000+ customers across 17 verticals including retail, manufacturing, warehousing, construction, and healthcare.

The product turns any on-prem camera into an AI-powered sensor: a unified dashboard that lets non-technical operators detect incidents, deter threats, and investigate in seconds instead of hours.

Challenge

The problem.

Spot AI had the product. What it didn't have was the digital footprint to match. Heading into Q4 2025, three things were working against them:

  1. AI chats were eating search. Buyers were starting their research in ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and Copilot — and the answers those engines gave were shaped by who had earned citations, not who had earned ad spend. Across Spot AI's most valuable query set, Verkada and Avigilon were dominating.
  2. The blog couldn't keep pace. A single in-house writer plus manual CMS work meant publishing velocity was tied to one person's calendar. New product releases, new use cases, and new verticals were shipping faster than the content team could document them.
  3. The traffic floor was falling. Broader SaaS blog traffic was dropping 20–40% in 2025 as zero-click AI answers replaced organic clicks (Kellogg Insight). Standing still meant going backward.

Spot AI's leadership needed one thing: the content engine — and the measurement layer — that would make them the brand AI chat engines reached for first.

What we built

The approach.

Apex built an AI-native content program designed for the AEO era — content that both earns Google rankings and gets cited in AI-generated answers. Three pillars, run in parallel.

1. An AI-powered content engine, tuned for citations

Most AI-generated content reads generic and earns nothing. Apex built the opposite: a production system calibrated to Spot AI's voice, ICP, and proof points. The workflow — built and run inside AirOps — layered in:

  • ICP-grounded messaging. Deep persona research plus language analysis of sales transcripts and ICP websites, so every article spoke in the vocabulary LP Directors, District Managers, and EHS leaders actually use.
  • Brand voice matching. Style analysis against Spot AI's PMM team's writing and original manual blog posts — replicated at scale, not approximated.
  • Social proof woven in. Customer quotes pulled from G2, Capterra, and Spot AI's own case studies, placed in CTA sections so every article closes with credibility, not a generic pitch.
  • Authority-building sources. Citations to industry associations, government data, and primary research — the kind of sources AI engines weight heavily when deciding who to cite.
  • FAQ schema and technical SEO hygiene. Structured data and on-page signals tuned for LLM readability.
  • Systematic internal linking. Every new piece connects to every relevant existing piece, compounding topical authority automatically.

Content flowed from AirOps straight into Webflow via a custom publishing connector — no manual CMS work, no copy-paste bottleneck.

2. A dedicated AEO measurement layer

You can't improve what you can't see. Apex stood up a custom AEO visibility tool that, every day, runs 200 ICP-relevant prompts across 5 major AI engines (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, and Copilot) and tracks:

  • Spot AI's citation rate per topic per engine
  • Share of voice vs. 28+ named competitors (Verkada, Avigilon, Rhombus, Eagle Eye, Voxel, Genetec, Cisco Meraki, and others)
  • Topic-level leadership (Retail Loss Prevention, Construction Safety, Manufacturing Efficiency, Workplace Safety, and more)

The measurement layer ran from day one, so every content sprint could be judged on whether it actually moved share of voice — not just whether articles went live.

3. A social listening and engagement layer

AEO performance isn't just a content signal — off-page signals matter. Apex layered in daily social monitoring across LinkedIn, X, and Reddit, surfacing 150+ monthly brand-relevant conversations with drafted engagement text ready for Spot AI's team to act on. The result: consistent authority signals, in public, in the right rooms.

Results

What happened.

1. #1 share of voice in AI engines — overtaking the market leaders

October 2025 (baseline): Spot AI ranked below Verkada and Avigilon in both mention rate and share of voice across the 200 tracked AI prompts.

January 2026: Spot AI became the #1 most mentioned and cited brand across all five tracked engines.

February 2026: The lead widened. Spot AI's citation rate climbed faster than any tracked competitor across every Retail Loss Prevention & Security category.

"In February, Spot AI effectively owned the Retail Safety conversation in AI chats. The surge in citations and mention rates locked in the top spot and widened the gap to the rest of the market."

2. 120% blog traffic growth — in a shrinking market

Since April 2025, total traffic to the Spot AI blog grew 120%, while broader SaaS industry traffic shrank by 20–40% due to AI chat cannibalization. Put differently: Spot AI didn't just survive the AI search transition — they compounded through it.

Takeaways

Why it worked.

  1. AEO is a content quality game, not a volume game. Generic AI output doesn't get cited. Apex's workflow layers — ICP language, brand voice, social proof, authoritative sourcing, schema — are what the AI engines actually reward.
  2. Measurement came first. Tracking 200 prompts daily across 5 engines meant every sprint was evaluated on share of voice, not publishing count. Quality issues surfaced in days, not quarters.
  3. Compounding beats spiking. The October → February transformation was sequential, not a one-time push. Each monthly batch built on the last — more articles, better schema, more authority, stronger internal linking, higher citation rates.
  4. The paid and organic loops were connected. February's content batch was informed by analysis of 500+ of Spot AI's own LinkedIn ads, so the organic voice matched the paid message. Buyers encountering Spot AI in either channel heard a consistent story.
  5. Swimming against the tide is the fastest way to pull ahead. While competitors' blogs bled traffic, Spot AI grew. The gap opens fastest when the market is contracting.
