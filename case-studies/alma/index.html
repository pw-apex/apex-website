Rebuilding paid media for a 30× LinkedIn SQL lift.

Background

The client.

Alma is a technology-enabled U.S. immigration legal services company serving two audiences: high-skill individuals (founders, researchers, technologists) pursuing O-1, EB-1A, and EB-2 NIW pathways, and businesses (HR, Mobility, Legal Ops) managing immigration programs at scale.

The brand's promise is built on three pillars — speed, excellence, and care — backed by a 2-week document turnaround and a 99%+ self-reported approval rate. Customers describe Alma as "responsive within minutes," "efficient," and a "true partner." That language matters for what worked later.

Challenge

Why they called us.

In late January 2026, Alma was spending ~$20K/month on paid media with limited ROI visibility. Three issues compounded: conversion events weren't firing reliably into HubSpot, so ads platforms couldn't learn and leadership couldn't defend the spend. The B2C Non-Brand campaign was over-consolidated — H-1B volume was dominating a single Smart Bidding pool and starving higher-margin visa categories like O-1 and EB-1/2/3 of budget. And the enterprise HR/Mobility buyer had no dedicated structure at all.

The brief: fix the foundation, then scale the channels that actually produce pipeline. Q1 target — grow SQLs from ~400/month toward 1,200/month to support a $1.8M new-business goal.

What we built

How we rebuilt the program.

Weeks 1–2 were foundation: a full HubSpot ↔ ads-stack audit, tracking fixes with an implementation partner, a brand kit and ICP-aligned ad personas, and a restructure plan splitting B2C Non-Brand into Tier 1 (O-1, EB-1/2/3, E-2 — higher margin, higher intent) and Tier 2 (H-1B, L-1, TN — volume-driven), plus a dedicated B2B campaign for HR/Mobility keywords.

Week 3 launched it all: Brand, NB Tier 1 (B2C), NB Tier 2 (B2C), and NB B2B as separate Google campaigns with dedicated budgets, Smart Bidding targets, and HubSpot-synced conversions. LinkedIn Conversion and Engagement groups went live in parallel, including a test of "Aizada Posts" — organic testimonial-style sponsored content featuring Alma's founder.

Weeks 4+ were optimization and expansion: an O-1A/O-1B ad-group split with dedicated landing pages (Tier 1 Quality Score trended 4.25 → 4.51 over 10 weeks), an AI-assisted search-term report categorizing 4,000 queries into Keep / Negative / Competitor / Brand / Review, TAM audiences uploaded across Google and LinkedIn as Customer Match / Company Lists with a +50% B2B bid modifier, three HR-focused and three enterprise-focused B2B creatives, a competitor conquest campaign against Manifest Law / Boundless / Fragomen / Envoy / BDV, a retargeting layer against 30-day web visitors and LinkedIn followers, and a LinkedIn lead magnet with three TAM-list variants.

Results

What happened.

Google Brand became the efficiency engine. Lead volume grew from 89 (Feb) to a projected 155 (April) — a 74% increase — while CPL fell from $15 to $8. In February, Brand alone drove 4 customers at a CAC of $307. Non-Brand improved on the efficiency metrics too: CPL from $122 (March) to a projected $62 (April), with Tier 1 Quality Score climbing 4.25 → 4.43 → 4.51.

LinkedIn was the breakout. SQLs went from 1 in February ($518 cost/SQL) to 13 in March ($252) to a projected 30 in April ($172) — 30× volume at 67% lower cost per SQL. "Aizada Posts" — testimonial content grounded in the founder's personal brand — ran at 11–14% CTR versus LinkedIn's 0.4–0.6% average, and in a single week delivered 51 SQLs at ~$10 each. A retargeting layer against 30-day web visitors and page followers produced the most efficient source in the entire account that week at $50/MQL.

B2B is early but promising. Google B2B launched March 17 and has already delivered its most efficient ad group — $42 cost per MQL on "Immigration Compliance" terms. The new NB B2B Tier 1 structure (Global Mobility, Compliance, Tracking & Reporting in separate ad groups) went live in April and is still in learning. LinkedIn B2B lead magnets produced 2 post-click MQLs at $91 each in the first days.

The honest gaps.

SQL → Opportunity in the Google funnel. Leads and SQLs are being generated; the blockage sits between SQL and Opp. Apex is surfacing individual contacts to Alma's sales team to understand whether timing, messaging, or qualification is the bottleneck. Until this clears, NB cost-per-customer will look worse than it should.

LinkedIn inbox handoff. The Engagement campaign is generating ~10 organic DMs per day to Alma's company page. Roughly 84% are replied to — but ~14 high-signal leads in the sample analyzed went unanswered, including two explicit complaints about earlier form submissions. Ads are creating inbound interest faster than intake is absorbing it.

Competitors Quality Score ramp. The conquest campaign launched with a ~1.3 Quality Score — expected for a new competitor build, but it drives up CPC and lowers impression share. Ad copy is being tailored per-competitor to pull relevance up.
