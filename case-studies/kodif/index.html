Background

The client.

Kodif is an AI-native customer-support platform. When they came to Apex they had a strong product, a small but loyal user base, and essentially no organic presence.

Every deal came from outbound, network, or paid. They needed a compounding channel — fast.

Challenge

Why they called us.

Kodif's team was lean and technical. Nobody wanted to spend the next year grinding out SEO articles. And they'd seen what generic AI-generated content did for other startups — nothing.

They needed authority content specific to the support-ops category, at a volume and cost that made SEO a real channel rather than a side project.

What we built

The hubs.

Apex redesigned the marketing site, built two content hubs (integrations and comparisons), and stood up a drafting pipeline tuned to the technical support-ops audience. We also shipped a programmatic layer for integration pages and competitor comparisons.

Average all-in cost per page — including infra, model costs, editor review, and publishing — came out under $4.

Results

What happened.

200+ pages live in the first six weeks. First page-one rankings started appearing in week six. Inside a quarter, organic traffic was 5× baseline and compounding.

Sales started getting inbound leads citing specific Kodif articles. The content hub became the entry point for the majority of new qualified pipeline.

"Apex took us from zero to authority. They redesigned our site, built two content hubs, and we're already ranking for high-intent searches."
